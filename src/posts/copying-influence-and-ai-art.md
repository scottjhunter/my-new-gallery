  <p>
    This kind of experiment illustrates why artists have every right to be concerned about people copying their work. It is too easy.
  </p>

  <p>
    These images are not copyrighted originals, and I still would never put them on a t-shirt to sell to Crater Lake lovers, because making money off other people's work feels wrong to me.
  </p>

  <p>
    The real question is where AI moves from "copying" to "being influenced by." Wherever that line is, humans should be held to the same standard.
  </p>

  <p>
    I can read a lot of Hemingway and then write a book that is deeply influenced by him. At what point does influence cross the line into appropriation?
  </p>
